A **continued fraction** is usually represented as $$b_0+\frac{a_1}{b_1+}\frac{a_2}{b_2+}\frac{a_3}{b_3+}\cdots\quad\text{or}\quad b_0+\cfrac{a_1}{b_1+\cfrac{a_2}{b_2+\cdots}}\quad\text{or}\quad b_0+\K_{i\in\mathbb Z}\frac{a_i}{b_i}$$where $a_n$ are **partial numerators**, $b_n$ **partial denominators**, and $x_n=b_0+\K_{i=1=}^n\frac{a_i}{b_i}=\frac{A_n}{B_n}$ the **$n$-th convergent**. We sometimes call the $A_n,B_n$ as **continuants**. It's assumed that $a_i\neq0$ (otherwise it's just a finite fraction), and or those $a_i=1,\forall i\in\mathbb Z$ we call it a **simple continued fraction**, and denote it as $[b_0;b_1,b_2,\cdots]$.
- **Recurrence of continuants**: we have the following matrix representation of recurrence $$\begin{pmatrix}A_n\\B_n\end{pmatrix}=\begin{pmatrix}A_{n-1}&A_{n-2}\\B_{n-1}&B_{n-2}\end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix}b_n\\ a_n\end{pmatrix}$$with initial values $A_{-1}=1,A_0=b_0,B_{-1}=0,B_0=1$.
	- **Determinant formula**: we have $A_{n-1}B_n-A_nB_{n-1}=\prod_{i=1}^n(-a_i)$.
- **Equivalence transform**: for any infinite non-zero sequence $\set{c_i}$ we have $$b_0+\K_{i\in\mathbb Z}\frac{a_i}{b_i}=b_0+\K_{i\in\mathbb Z}\frac{c_1\cdots c_ia_i}{c_ib_i}$$thus any general continued fraction can be transformed into a simple one, and when $b_i\neq0$ we may also transform it into a fraction with $b_i=1$.
