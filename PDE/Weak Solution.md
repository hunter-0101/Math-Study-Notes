Motivated by derivative of [[Distribution]], we define the **adjoint** ([[Operator on Hilbert Space]]) of a linear differential operator ([[Linear PDE]]) $L$ as $$L=\sum_{|\alpha|\le n}a_\alpha\partial^\alpha\imply L^*=\sum_{|\alpha|\le n}(-1)^{|\alpha|}\overline{a_\alpha}\partial^\alpha$$By integration by parts ([[Integral Calculus on Euclidean Space]]) we can verify that $$\braket{L\varphi,\psi}=\braket{\varphi,L^*\psi},\quad\forall\varphi,\psi\in C_0^\infty(\Omega)$$We say that $u$ is a **weak solution** of $Lu=f$ if $$\braket{f,\psi}=\braket{u,L^*\psi},\quad\forall \psi\in C_0^\infty(\Omega)$$Its significance is that this loosened conditions allows "solutions" that are not differentiable, providing more flexibilities.
- **Existence of weak solution**: given $\Omega\subset\mathbb R^d$ a bounded open subset and $L$ a linear differential operator, there exists $K\in\mathcal B(L^2(\Omega))$ s.t. $L(Kf)=f$ in the weak sense whenever $f\in L^2(\Omega)$.
  **Proof**: we need the inequality $\|L^*\psi\|_2\ge c\|\psi\|_2$ proved below. Consider a pre-Hilbert space $$\mathcal H_0=C_0^\infty(\Omega),\quad\braket{\varphi,\psi}_{\mathcal H_0}=\braket{L^*\varphi,L^*\psi}_{L^2}$$Let $\mathcal H$ be its completion ([[Hilbert Space]]), then since $\|\psi\|_{\mathcal H_0}=\|L^*\psi\|_2\ge c\|\psi\|_2$ we know that any Cauchy sequence of $\mathcal H$ is also a Cauchy sequence of $L^2(\Omega)$, hence we can identity $\mathcal H$ with a subspace of $L^2(\Omega)$. Also, $L^*\in\mathcal B(\mathcal H_0,L^2(\Omega))$ extends uniquely to $\mathcal H$ ([[FT on L2]]). Now given a fixed $f\in L^2(\Omega)$ consider the functional $$\ell_0:L^2(\Omega)\to\mathbb C,\quad\ell_0(\psi)=\braket{\psi,f}_{\mathcal H_0},\quad\forall\psi\in C_0^\infty(\Omega)$$by Cauchy-Schwarz inequality we have $$\begin{align}|\ell_0(\psi)|=\left|\braket{\psi,f}\right|&\le\|\psi\|_2\|f\|_2\\&\le c^{-1}\|L^*\psi\|_2\|f\|_2\\&\le c'\|\psi\|_0\end{align}$$where $c'=c^{-1}\|f\|_2$, hence $\ell_0$ is bounded, and can be extended uniquely to $\ell\in\mathcal B(\mathcal H,\mathbb C)$ with $\|\ell\|\le c^{-1}\|f\|_2$. By Riesz representation theorem ([[Topological Dual Space]]) there exists $U\in\mathcal H$ s.t. $$\ell(\psi)=\braket{\psi,U}_{\mathcal H}=\braket{L^*\psi,L^*U}_2,\quad\forall \psi\in C_0^\infty(\Omega)$$Denote $u=L^*U\in L^2(\Omega)$, then $\ell(\psi)=\braket{\psi,f}=\braket{L^*\psi,u}$, hence by definition $u$ is a weak solution of $Lu=f$. Let $Kf=u$, then $Kf$ is uniquely determined by $f$, and $$\|Kf\|_2=\|u\|_2=\|L^*U\|+2=\|U\|_0\le c^{-1}\|f\|_2$$hence $K\in\mathcal B(L^2(\Omega))$.
	- **Proof of $\|L^*\psi\|_2\ge c\|\psi\|_2$**: 
	- **Intuition**: issue of existence of weak solution boils down to whether $f\in\im L$. Since $(\im L)^\perp=\ker L^*$, what we essentially wanna show is $\ker L^*=\set{0}$, i.e., $L^*$ is lower bounded ([[Operator on Hilbert Space]]). The rest is some standard application of completion, extension theorem, and Riesz representation theorem.
