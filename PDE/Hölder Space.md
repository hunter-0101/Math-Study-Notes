Given domain $\Omega\subset\mathbb R^d$ define the **$\gamma$-Hölder seminorm** of a function $f$ as $$|f|_{C^{0,\gamma}(\overline\Omega)}=\sup_{x,y\in \Omega}\frac{|f(x)-f(y)|}{|x-y|^\gamma}$$which is obviously a seminorm ([[Vector Space]]), and the **$\gamma$-Hölder norm** as $$\|f\|_{C^{k,\gamma}(\overline\Omega)}=\sum_{|\alpha|\le k}\|\partial^\alpha f\|_{\infty}+\sum_{|\alpha|=k}|\partial^\alpha f|_{C^{0,\gamma}(\overline\Omega)}$$The **Hölder space** is defined as $$C^{k,\gamma}(\overline\Omega)=\{f\in C^k(\overline\Omega):\|f\|_{C^{k,\gamma}(\overline\Omega)}<\infty\}$$Related info on Hölder continuity can be found in [[Function on Euclidean Space]].
- **[[Banach Space]] structure**: $C^{k,\gamma}(\overline\Omega)$ is Banach under the $\gamma$-Hölder norm.
  **Proof**: the first term in $\|\cdot\|_{C^{k,\gamma}(\overline\Omega)}$ is actually the norm $\|\cdot\|_{C^k(\overline\Omega)}$ in [[Differential Calculus on Euclidean Space]], while each $|\partial^\alpha\cdot|_{C^{0,\gamma}(\overline\Omega)}$ is a seminorm by linearity of [[Differential Calculus on Euclidean Space]], hence $\|\cdot\|_{C^{k,\gamma}(\overline\Omega)}$ is a norm ([[Vector Space]]). For completeness, let $$f_n\xto{C^k(\overline\Omega)}f,\quad\|\cdot\|_{C^k(\overline\Omega)}=\sup_{|\alpha|=k}|\partial^\alpha f|$$where we used completeness of $C^k(\overline\Omega)$, and denote $g_n=\partial^\alpha f_n,f=\partial^\alpha f$, then $$\frac{|g(x)-g(y)|}{|x-y|^\gamma}\le\frac{|g(x)-g_n(x)|+|g_n(x)-g_n(y)|+|g_n(y)-g(y)|}{|x-y|^\gamma}\le M_\alpha$$which follows from Cauchy-ness and convergence. Hence $f\in C^{k,\gamma}(\overline\Omega)$, and further $$\frac{|(g-g_n)(x)-(g-g_n)(y)|}{|x-y|^\gamma}=\lim_{k\to\infty}\frac{|(g_k-g_n)(x)-(g_k-g_n)(y)|}{|x-y|^\gamma}$$where RHS can be made arbitrarily small independent of $x\neq y$, hence $f_n\xto{C^{0,\gamma}(\overline\Omega)}f$. Since there are only finitely many $|\alpha|\le k$, we conclude that $$f_n\xto{C^{k,\gamma}(\overline\Omega)}f\in C^{k,\gamma}(\overline\Omega)$$Hence $C^{k,\gamma}(\overline\Omega)$ is Banach.
