**Intuition**: [[Category]] theory emphasizes that objects should be understood by their relations to other objects rather than defining them on their own. The Yoneda lemma motivates this perspective.

---
**Yoneda lemma**: for any covariant $F:\mathcal C\to\textbf{Set}$ and any $A\in\ob(\mathcal C)$ there is a natural bijection $$\nat(h_A,F)\cong F(A),\quad \varphi\leftrightarrow\varphi_A(\id_A)$$**Proof**: take $\varphi\in\nat(h_A,F)$ where we denote $\varphi_X:h_A(X)\to F(X)$ for $X\in\ob(\mathcal C)$, then by definition ([[Category]]) for any $X,Y\in\ob(\mathcal C),f\in\mor_\mathcal C(X,Y)$ the following diagram commutes:

- **Dual statement**: the dual statement of Yoneda lemma also holds, which uses contravariant $F:\mathcal C\to\textbf{Set}$ and the contravariant hom functor instead: $\nat(h^A,F)\cong F(A)$. 
- **Intuition**: Yoneda lemma essentially states that natural transformations from $\hom(A,-)$ into any set-valued functor $F$ are entirely determined by the image of the identity morphism $\id_A\in\mor_\mathcal C(A,A)$.
- **Yoneda embedding**: the (covariant) Yoneda embedding for $\mathcal C$ is the covariant functor $$Y:\mathcal C\to[\mathcal C^{\text{op}},\textbf{Set}],\quad\begin{cases}A\mapsto h^A,&\forall A\in\ob(\mathcal C) \\ f\mapsto(\varphi:h^A\to h^B),&\forall f\in\mor_{\mathcal C}(A,B)\end{cases}$$We have that Yoneda embedding functor is fully faithful.
  **Proof**:  take $A,B\in\ob(\mathcal C),F=h^B$, then by the dual statement of Yoneda lemma we have $$\nat(h^A,h^B)\cong h^B(A)=\mor_\mathcal C(A,B)$$By Yoneda lemma any $\varphi\in\nat(h^A,h^B)$, is uniquely correlated to $f=\varphi_A(\id_A)\in\mor_\mathcal C(A,B)$. This is a natural bijection from $\mor_\mathcal C(A,B)$ to $\nat(h^A,h^B)$ for every $A,B\in\ob(\mathcal C)$, hence the Yoneda embedding is fully faithful.
	- **Dual statement**: there is also a contravariant Yoneda embedding functor $\mathcal C^{\text{op}}\to[\mathcal C,\textbf{Set}]$, but that's simply the covariant functor applied to $\mathcal C^\text{op}$.
	- **Generalization of Cayley's theorem**: Cayley's theorem (discussed below) says that any group is isomorphic to a subgroup of symmetric group of the group itself. Similarly Yoneda embedding says that any category is a subcategory of presheaf on the category itself.
## Examples
- **Dedekind cut**: view the poset ([[Order]]) $(\mathbb R,\le)$ as a category with $a\to b\iff a\le b$, then $$h_r(s)=\mor_\mathbb R(r,s)=(r\le s),\quad h_r(s\to k)=(r\le s)\circ(s\le k)=(r\le k)$$Now the Yoneda lemma says that any object is determined up to isomorphism by objects that are larger than or equal to it, which is exactly the Dedekind cut definition of [[Real Number System]].
- **Cayley's theorem**: see [[Group]] for the setup. We now have a isomorphism $$\nat(h_*,F)\cong F(*)$$Applying this to the identity functor $G=\mor_*(*,*)$ we get $$\nat(G,G)\cong G$$Further by the Yoneda embedding we have $$*_G\hookrightarrow[*_G^{\text{op}},\textbf{Set}]$$which embeds $G$ into the category of $G$-sets, which is essentially Cayley's theorem ([[Group Actions]])
- **Matrix:** consider the category $\textbf{Vect}_k$. The hom functor $h_V:\textbf{Vect}_k\to\textbf{Set}$ sends each $W\in\textbf{Vect}_k$ to the set of linear maps $V\to W$, and the Yoneda lemma tells that $$\nat(h_V,F)\cong F(V)$$Hence a natural functor $\varphi\in\nat(h_V,F)$ is determined by $F(V)$. Intuitively it tells that a transformation is fully captured by how it behaves on maps out of $V$.
- **Riesz representation theorem**: a [[Hilbert Space]] $H$ is equipped with an inner product, which is a continuous linear map $\braket{-,-}:H^\text{op}\otimes H\to\mathbb C$ ($H^\text{op}$ is $H$ with order of inner product flipped around). This can be identified as a continuous linear map from $H^\text{op}$ to set of linear maps $\mathcal L(H,\mathbb C)$. The RRT states that this mapping is isomorphic. Categorically this happens when $H^\text{op}$ is equivalent to a presheaf category, which is exactly true in the case.
