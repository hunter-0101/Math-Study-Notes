Given a [[Category]] $\mathcal C$, a **diagram** of shape $J$, where $J$ is another category, is a functor $$F:J\to\mathcal C$$Here $J$ is called the **index category** or **scheme** of the diagram $F$. A **cone** to $F$ is an object $N\in\ob(\mathcal C)$ together with a family $\psi$ of morphisms $$\psi_X:N\to F(X),\quad X\in\ob(J)$$s.t. every morphism $f:\in\mor_J(X,Y)$ we have $F(f)\circ\psi_X=\psi_Y$. A **limit** of $F$ is a cone $(L,\phi)$ to $F$ s.t. for every cone $(N,\psi)$ to $F$ there exists a **unique** morphism $u:N\to L$ s.t. $\phi_X\circ u=\psi_X$.
