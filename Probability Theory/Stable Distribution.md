A non-degenerated [[Distribution Function]] $F$ is **stable** if given independent $X,X_1,X_2\sim F$, for any $a,b>0$ there exists $c>0,d\in\mathbb R$ s.t. $$aX_1+bX_2\sim cX+d$$and it's **strictly stable** if $d=0$. The family of stable distributions, via theory of [[Convolution]] and [[FT on L2]], can be represented by its characteristic function ([[Random Variable]]) as $$\varphi(t;\alpha,\beta,c,\mu)=\exp(it\mu-|ct|^\alpha(1-i\beta\sgn(t)\Phi))\where\Phi=\begin{cases}\tan\left(\dfrac{\pi\alpha}{2}\right),&\alpha\neq1\\-\dfrac{2}{\pi}\ln|t|,&\alpha=1\end{cases}$$here $\mu\in\mathbb R$ is the **location parameter**, $\beta\in[-1,1]$ is the **skewness parameter**, $c\in(0,\infty)$ the **scale parameter**, and $\alpha\in(0,2]$ the **stability parameter**. 

## Lévy $\alpha$-stable distribution
The PDF of the **Lévy $\alpha$-stable distribution** (abbreviated as **Lévy distribution**) is $$f(x;\mu,c)=\sqrt{\frac{c}{2\pi}}\frac{\exp\left(-\dfrac{c}{2(x-\mu)}\right)}{(x-\mu)^{3/2}}$$It's a specific realization of stable distribution that serves important role in the generalized CLT.
**Generalized central limit theorem (GCLT)**: a non-degenerate RV $Z$ is $\alpha$-stable for some $\alpha\in(0,2]$ iff there is an i.i.d sequence $X_i$ and $a_n>0,b_n\in\mathbb R$ s.t. $$a_n\sum_{i=1}^nX_i-b_n\xto d Z$$
