Given a set $S$ we suppose for every $s\in S$ there is an "inverse" $s^{-1}\in S^{-1}$, and define 
- **Letters**: the set of letters is given by $T=S\cup S^{-1}$. 
- **Word**: a word in $S$ is any written product of letters in $T$, i.e., that is, an element of the monoid ([[Group]]) generated by $T$. 
	- **Reduced word**: a word is **reduced** if there is no neighboring pair that can be canceled. By induction on length we can show that there is only one reduced form of a word.
	- **Equivalent words**: two words are equivalent if their reduced form are the same. 
The **free group** $F_S$ with **free generating set** $S$ consists of all equivalence classes ([[Set Theory]]) of words. $S$ is called the **generator** of $F_S$, and $|S|$ is called the **rank** of $F_S$. ## Group presentation
Formally, given a set $S$ and let $F_S$ be the free group on $S$, and let $R$ be the set of some words on $S$ then $R$ naturally gives a subset of $F_S$. Consider the normal closure $N$ of $R$, we define the group by its presentation$$\langle S|R\rangle=F_S/N$$Here $S$ is the **generator**, and $R$ is the **relator**.
- **Normal closure**: normal closure of a set $S$, denoted $\mathrm{ncl}_G(S)$, is the smallest normal subgroup containing the set. By the definition of normal subgroup we know that $$\mathrm{ncl}_G(S)=\langle S^G\rangle=\langle \{g^{-1}sg:g\in G\}\rangle$$
- **Absolute presentation**: in $G\cong\langle S|R\rangle$ there is a tacit assumption that $G$ is the "freest" group satisfying the generators and relators. To eliminate the tacit assumption we consider to specify the **irrelators** $I$, satisfying $i\neq1,\forall i\in I$. Define $$G=\langle S|R,I\rangle$$such that $G$ has the presentation $G=\langle S|R\rangle$, and given any homomorphism $\phi:G\to H$ such that $I$ are satisfied in $\phi(G)$, $G$ is isomorphic to $\phi(G)$.
	- An equivalent condition is for any non-trivial $N\triangleleft G$, we have $I\cap N\supsetneq\{1\}$.
