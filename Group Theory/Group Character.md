For a [[Group Representation]] $(\rho,V)$ where $V$ is a finite-dimensional [[Vector Space]] over $\mathbb C$ for finite [[Group]] $G$, define the character of $g\in G$ on $V$ as $$\chi_V(g)=\tr\rho(g)$$Notice that $\chi_V(h^{-1}gh)=\chi_V(g)$ by [[Vector Space]], hence $\chi$ is a class function, i.e., invariant within a conjugate class. - **Arithmetic operations**: for two representations $(\rho,V),(\pi,W)$ we have $$\begin{align}\chi_{V\oplus W}&=\chi_V+\chi_W\\\chi_{V\otimes W}&=\chi _V\cdot\chi_W\\\chi_{V^*}&=\overline{\chi_V}\\\chi_{V\wedge V}(g)&=\frac{1}{2}\left(\chi_V(g)^2-\chi_V(g^2)\right)\end{align}$$**Proof**: the first two lines follows from the fact that if $\set{\lambda_i},\set{\mu_j}$ are eigenvalues of $g$ in $V,W$, then $\set{\lambda_i}\cup\set{\mu_j},\set{\lambda_i\mu_j}$ are eigenvalues in $V\oplus W$ and $V\otimes W$ ([[Eigendecomposition]]). The third line follows from definition of $\rho^*$ ([[Group Representation]]) since all eigenvalues are $|g|$-th root of unity, and the fourth from the equality $$\sum_{i<j}\lambda_i\lambda_j=\frac{1}{2}\left(\left(\sum_i\lambda_i\right)^2-\sum_i\lambda_i^2\right)$$and the fact that $g^2$ has eigenvalues $\set{\lambda_i^2}$.

- **Calculation of eigenvalues**: when $\chi_V$ is known we immediately know eigenvalues $\lambda_i$ of $g$, since by definition we have $$\chi_V(g^k)=\tr\rho(g^k)=\sum_i\lambda_i^k,\quad\forall 1\le k\le|g|$$By Vieta's formula ([[Polynomial Ring]]) this gives rise to values of each $\lambda_i$.
## Projection formula
- **Invariant subspace**: given a representation $(\rho,V)$ denote $$V^G=\set{v\in V:gv=v,\forall g\in G}$$Then the the projection of $V$ onto $V^G$ is given by $$\varphi=\frac{1}{|G|}\sum_{g\in G}g$$**Proof**: it can be easily verified that $\im\varphi\subset V^G,\varphi^2=\varphi$, while surjectivity is also obvious.
	- **Trivial subrepresentations**: the number $m$ of copies of trivial subrepresentations of a given representation is given by $$m=\dim V^G=\tr\varphi=\frac{1}{|G|}\chi_V(g)$$since characters of any irreducible representation other than the trivial ones sum up to $0$.
- **Orthogonality lemma**: consider $\hom(V,W)$ as a [[Group Representation]], and denote $$\hom(V,W)^G=\set{G\text{-linear maps from }V\text{ to }W}$$By Schur's lemma ([[Group Representation]]) when $V$ is irreducible $\dim\hom(V,W)^G$ is the multiplicity of $V$ in $W$, and vise versa, while when both $V,W$ are irreducible we have $$\dim\hom(V,W)^G=\begin{cases}1,&V\cong W\\0,&V\not\cong W\end{cases}$$Meanwhile, by the arithmetic properties we have $$\hom(V,W)=V^*\otimes W\quad\Longrightarrow\quad\chi_{\hom(V,W)}=\overline{\chi_V}\cdot\chi_W$$Hence substituting these to the above formula we get $$\frac{1}{|G|}\sum_{g\in G}\overline{\chi_V(g)}\chi_W(g)=\begin{cases}1,&V\cong W\\0,&V\not\cong W\end{cases}$$This motivates us to define a Hermitian inner product by $$\braket{\alpha,\beta}=\frac{1}{|G|}\sum_{g\in G}\overline{\alpha(g)}\beta(g),\quad\forall\alpha,\beta\in\mathbb C_\text{class}(G)=\set{\text{class functions on }G}$$
	- **Basis property**: any representation is determined by its character, since by arithmetic properties we gave $$V=\bigoplus_{i=1}^kV_i^{\oplus a_i}\quad\Longrightarrow\quad\chi_V=\sum_{i=1}^ka_i\chi_{V_i}$$where $\chi_{V_i}$ are linearly independent. Further the coefficients are given by $a_i=\braket{\chi_V,\chi_{V_i}}$.
	- **Irreducibility criterion**: $V$ is irreducible iff $\braket{\chi_V,\chi_V}=1$, since by definition we have $$V=\bigoplus_{i=1}^kV_i^{\oplus a_i}\quad\Longrightarrow\quad\braket{\chi_V,\chi_V}=\sum_{i=1}^k\braket{\chi_{V_i},\chi_{V_i}}=\sum_{i=1}^ka_i^2$$The result follows immediately.
	- **Results on permutation representation**: for regular representation $R=\bigoplus V_i^{\oplus a_i}$ since $$\chi_R(g)=\begin{cases}0,&g\neq e\\|G|,&g=e\end{cases}\quad\Longrightarrow\quad a_i=\braket{\chi_{V_i},\chi_R}=\frac{1}{|G|}\chi_{V_i}(e)|G|=\dim V_i$$hence by definition ([[Group Representation]]) we have $$|G|=\sum_{i=1}^k(\dim V_i)^2,\quad 0=\sum_{i=1}^k(\dim V_i)\chi_{V_i}(g)\text{ if }g\neq e$$which amounts to the Fourier inversion formula of finite group.
