Below we denote $\textbf{Ban}$ the category of Banach spaces, with morphisms being linear contractions (i.e., operator norm no larger than 1). Given two Banach spaces $V,W$ denote $V\oplus_pW$ the direct sum with norm $$\|(v,w)\|_p=\left(\frac{\|v\|^p+\|w\|^p}{2}\right)^{1/p}$$Denote $\mathcal A^p$ the category of triples $(V,v,\delta)$ where $V$ is Banach, $\|v\|\le 1$ and $\delta:V\oplus_pV\to V$ a map of Banach spaces satisfying $\delta(v,v)=v$. The morphisms are the maps in $\textbf{Ban}$ that preserves the structure: $$\theta:(V,v,\delta)\to(V',v',\delta')\quad\text{s.t.}\quad\theta(v)=v',\quad\theta(\delta(v_1,v_2))=\delta(\theta(v_1),\theta(v_2)),\forall v_1,v_2\in V$$
**Theorem: universal property of $L^p([0,1])$**: for $1\le p<\infty$ define the map $$\gamma:L^p([0,1])\oplus L^p([0,1])\to L^p([0,1]),\quad\gamma(f,g)(x)=\begin{cases}f(2x),&x<1/2\\g(2x-1),&x>1/2\end{cases}$$then $(L^p([0,1]),1,\gamma)$ is the initial object of $\mathcal A^p$.
