Given $(X,\mu)$ a [[Measure]] space and $\Phi(t)$ a continuous, convex ([[Convexity]]), increasing function on $[0,\infty)$, then the **Orlicz space** induced by $\Phi(t)$ is define as $$L^\Phi=\left\{f\text{ measurable}:\exists M>0\text{ s.t. }\int_X\Phi\left(\frac{|f(x)|}{M}\right)d\mu<\infty\right\}$$together with a norm on the space given by $$\|f\|_\Phi=\inf\Set{M>0:\int_X\Phi\left(\frac{|f(x)|}{M}\right)d\mu\le1},\quad$$we have that $\|\cdot\|_\Phi$ is a norm that makes $L^\Phi$ a [[Banach Space]]. Notice that the [[Lp Space]] is a special case with $L^\Phi=L^p\iff\Phi=t^p$.
- For $1\le p<q<\infty$ define $$\Phi(t)=\begin{cases}t^p,& 0\le t\le 1\\ t^q,&t\ge1\end{cases}$$then the space $L^\Phi$ together with the norm defined above is equivalent to $L^p+L^q$ defined above in the sense of norm equivalence.
- **Hölder-like inequality**: given $\varphi,\psi$ continuous, strictly increasing and mutual inverse to each other, denote their primitives as $$\Phi(x)=\int_0^x\varphi(t)dt,\quad\Psi(x)=\int_0^x\psi(t)dt$$and consider the Orlicz spaces $L^\Phi,L^\psi$, we have the Hölder-like inequality $$\int|fg|\le C\|f\|_{\Phi}\|g\|_\Psi,\quad C>0,\forall f\in L^\Phi,g\in L^\Psi$$and further if there exists $c>0$ s.t. $\Phi(2t)\le c\Phi(t),\forall t\ge0$ then $(L^\Phi)^*\cong L^\Psi$.
