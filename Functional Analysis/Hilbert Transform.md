As discussed in [[Hardy Space]], given $f\in L^2(\mathbb R)$ we can define $$F(z)=\int_0^\infty \hat f(\xi)e^{2\pi i\xi z}d\xi=C(f)(z)=\frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_\mathbb R\frac{f(\zeta)}{\zeta-z}d\zeta,\quad\forall\Im z>0$$where $C(f)$ denotes [[Cauchy Integral]] over $\mathbb R$. If we define $$P(f)(x)=\int_\mathbb R\hat f(\xi)\chi_{(0,\infty)}(\xi)e^{2\pi i\xi x}d\xi$$then by Plancherel theorem ([[FT on L2]]) we find $F(x+iy)\xto{L^2}P(f),y\to0$. This motivates us to define the **Hilbert transform** as $$H(f)(x)=\int_\mathbb R\hat f(\xi)\frac{\sgn\xi}{i}e^{2\pi i\xi x}d\xi$$We have that $P=\frac{1}{2}(I+iH)$, and $H$ unitary on $L^2$ with $H^2=-I$.
- **Realization as singular integral**: for $f\in L^2(\mathbb R)$ we have that $$H(f)(x)=\frac{1}{\pi}\text{p.v.}\int_\mathbb R\frac{f(x-t)}{t}dt$$**Proof**: denote $\mathcal P,\mathcal Q$ the Poisson kernel pair ([[Convolution]]), then by Plancherel's theorem we have that $f*\mathcal Q_\varepsilon\xto{L^2}H(f),\varepsilon\to0$. Denote $H_\varepsilon(f)$ the above integral over $\set{|x|\ge\varepsilon}$, then $$H_\varepsilon(f)(x)-(f*\mathcal Q_\varepsilon)(x)=(f*\Delta_\varepsilon)(x)\quad\text{where}\quad\Delta_\varepsilon=\frac{\chi_{\set{|x|\ge\varepsilon}}}{\pi x}-\mathcal Q_\varepsilon$$Observe that $\Delta_\varepsilon(x)=\varepsilon^{-1}\Delta_1(\varepsilon^{-1}x)$ with $|\Delta_1(x)|\le\frac{A}{1+x^2}$, hence the family $\set{\Delta_\varepsilon}$ satisfies all conditions for approximation to the identity except for $\int\Delta_\varepsilon=0$, hence by [[Convolution#Good kernel]] we have $H_\varepsilon(f)\xto{L^2} f*\mathcal Q_\varepsilon,\varepsilon\to0$, which finally yields the desired limit.
- **Extension to [[Lp Space]]**: for $1<p<\infty$ the Hilbert transform $H$, initially defined on $L^2\cap L^p$, satisfies the inequality $$\|Hf\|_p\le A_p\|f\|_p,\quad\forall f\in L^2\cap L^p$$Hence by [[Operator]] it can be extended to all of $L^p$ satisfying the same bound.
  **Proof**: first notice that the inequality for $f:\mathbb R\to\mathbb R$ immediately implies that for $f:\mathbb R\to\mathbb C$ since given $f=f_R+if_I$ where $f_R,f_I:\mathbb R\to\mathbb R$ we have $$\|Hf\|_p\le \|Hf_R\|_p+\|Hf_I\|_p\le A_p(\|f_R\|_p+\|f_I\|_p)\le2A_p\left\|\sqrt{f_R^2+f_I^2}\right\|_p=2A_p\|f\|_p$$Another useful observation is that, for $f\in C_c^\infty(\mathbb R)$ its Cauchy integral $F(z)=C(f)$ extends as a continuous bounded function on $\mathbb R_+^2$ with $|F(z)|=O(\frac{1}{1+|z|}),\Im z\ge0$, which can be shown by its Fourier transform representation. Now utilizing the boundedness condition we have $$\int_\mathbb R(F(x))^kdx=0,\quad\forall k\in\mathbb N.k\ge2$$by [[Contour Integral]], and since $F(x)=f(x)+iHf(x)$, by taking $k=2$ we show that $H$ is unitary on $L^2$. For general $k=2l,l\in\mathbb N_{\ge2}$, since $$\Re(f+iHf)^{2l}=\sum_{r=0}^l(-1)^{l-r}\binom{2l}{2r}f^{2r}(Hf)^{2l-2r}$$we have $$\begin{align}\|Hf\|_{2l}^{2l}&=\int(Hf)^{2l}dx\\&\le\sum_{r=1}^l\binom{2l}{2r}\int f^{2r}(Hf)^{2l-2r}dx&\text{Direct estimation}\\&\le\sum_{r=1}^l\binom{2l}{2r}\|f\|_{2l}^{2r}\|Hf\|_{2l}^{2l-2r}&\text{Hölder's inequality}\end{align}$$Where the Hölder's inequality is applied on conjugate $\frac{2l}{2r},\frac{2l}{2l-2r}$. Set $\|f\|_{2l}=1$ and denote $X=\|Hf\|_{2l}$, then the above inequality can be written as $X^{2l}\le\sum_{r=1}^l\binom{2l}{2r}X^{2l-2r}$, which implies $X\le2^l$, hence we've proved the result on $C_c^\infty$ with $A_p=2^{p/2}$. Density argument ([[Lp Space]]) extends the results to simple functions on $L^2\cap L^p,p=2l$, hence [[Riesz Interpolation Theorem]] can be applied (on $p_0=q_0=2,p_1=q_1=2l$) to yields the inequality for all $2\le p\le 2l$. Finally the cases $1<p\le2$ can be shown by duality ([[Banach Space]]), with the simple identity $$\int_\mathbb R(Hf)\overline gdx=-\int_\mathbb Rf(\overline{Hg})dx,\quad\forall f,g\in L^2(\mathbb R)$$which is actually the Plancherel's identity ([[FT on L2]]) modified according to the definition of Hilbert transform. Now we've proved the result for simple $f\in L^p$ where $1<p<\infty$, and the standard mechanism yields the general result.
